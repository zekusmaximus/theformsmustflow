Original teaser text (not from the book)

The Gloridian invasion fleet had conquered nine hundred and forty-seven worlds without a single procedural delay. Their methods were elegant, their technology overwhelming, their efficiency absolute. A planet would be identified, assessed, and integrated into the Empire within a standard solar cycle. Resistance was futile. Negotiation was unnecessary. Bureaucracy was unheard of.

Earth was different.

It began with the environmental impact assessment. The Gloridian commander, whose name translated roughly to "Swift Unstoppable Victory," stood in what humans called a "waiting room" and tried to understand why his fleet's orbital positioning required approval from something called the County Planning Commission.

"But we are in space," he said, his translation device rendering his confusion as a polite cough. "Space is not in any county."

The clerk behind the counter—her badge read "Doris, Administrative Support II"—did not look up from her terminal. "Anything that can be seen from the county requires county approval. That's the ordinance. You can appeal to the regional board, but their next meeting isn't for three weeks."

About the Author

JZ

Jeffrey A. Zyjeski

Jeffrey A. Zyjeski writes satirical science fiction that finds the absurd in the familiar and the familiar in the absurd. "The Forms Must Flow" is his debut novel, exploring what happens when cosmic stakes collide with everyday institutional inertia.

He lives with an appreciation for the people who keep complex systems running—and a healthy suspicion of any process requiring more than three signatures.
